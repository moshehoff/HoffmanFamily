   This uncommon Jewish family name was my grandmother’s maiden name.     I have not found its origin  in any source, however  I learned that in Yiddish

a person who sits on a panel or in  judgment  is a besitser בעזיצער   from the word  “zits”  זיץ  to sit.  Thus a zitserman   זיצערמאן   or as it is sometimes

written –  zaitserman -  must have  been a dayan   דיין  

 a member of a rabbinical court.  In European languages constructions using the root words for  seats, sitting (and even thronus) such as chairman, assizes,

seat of goverment, a sitting, etc, are common.  

   Aaron Zitserman of Bershad married  Elka Vestil.

    A digression into these names:

I have the name Vestil  from the death certificates of two of his daughters  (Tob’l and Pessy)  where it is their mother’s family name.

The death certificate of Mariam notes that her mother was named Elka.

This we know to be true. It follows that Vestil can only be the family name.

Each of the three daughters – Tob’l, Pessy and Mariam - bore  a  daughter

named Elke – they were Alyce Hoffman, Olga Schwarz and Elke Greenberg

– in line with Jewish naming conventions. On his daughters’  death certificates Aaron was described as a business proprietor.

   Vestil: This rare Jewish family name appears to be unique to Russia and Moldavia. A search of the Jewish Genealogy databases and of the Shoah victims listed by Yad Vashem has not turned up much of relevance. Vestil is a diminutive of vest, and as such refers to the fringed undershirt worn by  ארנע ארבעה כנפות  religious Jewish men: ie: arba kanfot.

Myron turned up on my sister’s DNA. This is his cordial letter to us from 2020.

Dear Sandi, Peter and David,

    Thanks so much for taking some time to write to me.  The Hoffmans were always some news from my mother Sara as we were growing up and throughout her life.  I have vague recollections of being with some of you when my family lived in Perth for six months in 1955.  I think that I attended some sort of religious school on occasion or other event.

    Anyway, my wife Margie and I live on the island of Kauai in Hawaii.  We retired here from San Diego 11 years ago.  We never had children by choice.  We will be married 50 years this September. My sister Jenny and her husband Shelly Silver live in Cape Cod, Massachusetts and have a 31 year old son, Sam who usually lives in Brooklyn, N.Y. with his long time partner Allison.  They are staying with Jenny and Shelly while the pandemic settles down (if ever).  Jenny lived in Perth only for about six months or so with my parents in 1973 and not since. My brother Paul is long divorced and lives in Carlsbad, California.  He never had children either.  Our family tree is much simpler than yours! All three of us are retired now.

    We usually travel a great deal for pleasure but are stationary at his point in time, as you might suspect.  Being stuck on Kauai isn’t so bad and we are enjoying it.   I went to UCLA and California State for my bachelors degree and then to USC for an MBA.  I spent my working years in Real Estate and construction always working for myself. We lived in Southern and Central California all of our working years.

    My only living relative on Sara’s side is Michael Kemeny, Alka’s (Alice’s) husband, who is 94 and living at Montgomery House care facility in Mont Claremont, Perth.  Paul, Jenny and myself have visited him many times over the years, most recently last year.

    Please forward this on as you please.

All the very best, Myron
